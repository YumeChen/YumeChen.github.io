About

Hi, I am Meng Chen, go by Yume. I am Chinese and come to an America high school in Connecticut when I was 17 years old, and then I went to Seattle studied in college and a 4-year university, the University of Washington Bothell (UW Bothell) majoring in Interdisciplinary Art (IA).

I was born into an unordinary family compared to other families. My father was the breadwinner who made a living from city to city; on the other hand, my mother was the homemaker who never stayed at home, since she was addicted to gambling and mahjong. Because my parents were not always around, they left me alone at home to watch home decorating TV shows and read interior design magazines they bought. The only family memory for me was sticking around my father when he was doing carpentry work as his hobby at home. However, even those few memories were luxurious to me when I was sent to boarding school.

Strictness, discipline and uniformity are the most primary guides of many school in China. Most students focus on study math, physics and English, since those courses impact the quality of examination results which determine a successful future of students in the Chinese educational environment. Whereas, I am, and was had then different from most students. While other students were memorizing math and chemical formulas, I was drawing my own version of the classroom with enlarged windows for augmented natural lighting and with an oblique roof for diffuse sunlight, since the original classroom was too dark for many students who sat far away from the window.

After I went to America, and as I took more and more art related classes and my passion kept growing. Many inspirations of design are derived from different cultures, histories, religions and artistic forms which will widely apply to my career. I am interested to combine different cultures and different eras into a new interior design style. I believe UW Bothell and IA can help me to make my interest come true which can absorb higher educational knowledge for developing myself as a future interior designer.
